Example of Electricity generation through footsteps

Electricity generation through footsteps seems like a rising energy solution these days. However, countries like the Netherlands, Japan and the USA have had this energy solution for quite a long time now. In Rotterdam, Netherlands, two Dutch men came up with a practical use of dance moves. They designed a sustainable dance floor that produces electricity from the kinetic energy created by footsteps. The energy generated can easily power the nightclub’s digital system.

Pavegan, a London-based company, also came up with a similar idea. However, they focused on the daily footsteps of people. Kinetic energy created by the footsteps of pedestrians is converted into clean electricity. Among many other places, these tiles are installed outside the White House, Washington DC.

Power generating floors are also installed in Japan by Soundpower corporation outside Fujisawa City Hall in Kanagawa Prefecture and Shibuya Station in Tokyo. The company has also come up with two more ventures: sound power and vibration power generation. Through sound power, energy from speech and noise vibrations is converted into electricity. Which will allow people to charge their mobile phones and other applications by simply holding a conversation. Vibration-power generation converts the vibrational energy of pedestrians, bicycles, and automobiles into electricity.
